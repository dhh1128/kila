# People

**Scope**: Kinship terms, social roles, gender marking, family relationships, compositional principles

**Related files**: honorifics.md (address forms), glossary.md (people terms), morphology.md (gender prefixes)

---

Most people words have a form that doesn't imply any gender, which can then be combined with a *hi-* or *ci-* prefix to make an explicit gender claim. The core family terms for mother, father, brother, and sister (*mai*, *pai*, *hyb*, *cyb*) each combine the gender prefix with the root in a single syllable. These single-syllable gendered forms can appear as components in longer compound words, mixing gendered and neutral elements as needed. People who are well known to a speaker tend to be described with the short form.

## Compositional Principles

Kinship terms are built systematically by combining roots with prefixes. The patterns are completely regular and productive.

**Core roots:**
- *sai* = 'parent' (neutral) → *mai* = 'mother' (*ci-sai* fused), *pai* = 'father' (*hi-sai* fused)
- *syb* = 'sibling' (neutral) → *cyb* = 'sister' (*ci-syb* fused), *hyb* = 'brother' (*hi-syb* fused)
- *co* = 'child' (neutral) → *cico* = 'daughter', *hico* = 'son'
- *ant* = 'person' (neutral) → *ciant* = 'woman', *hiant* = 'man'
- *kuant* = 'family' (neutral)
- *knt* = 'kin/relation' (simplified form of *kuant*)

**Prefixes and their ordering:**

When multiple prefixes combine, they attach in this order: **number/generation > relationship-type > gender > root**

1. **Number/generation:** *ta-* 'adds a generation', *to-* 'two', *si-* 'three', *vx-* 'plural'
   - *tapai* = 'grandfather'
   - *tatasai* = 'great grandparent' 
   - *vxtasai* = 'direct-line ancestors (plural generations)'
   
2. **Relationship type:** *ku-* 'by marriage', *nir-* 'by close/honorary relationship', *lon-* 'far/distant'
   - *kusyb* = 'step-sibling'
   - *nirhico* = 'godson' (honorary male child)
   - *lonkuant* = 'tribe' (far family)
   
3. **Gender:** *hi-* 'masculine', *ci-* 'feminine'
   - *hico* = 'son'
   - *tahico* = 'grandson' (generation > gender > root)
   - *tacico* = 'granddaughter'

**Extended relations:**

Cousins and extended family use number prefixes to indicate generational distance:
- *takusyb* = '1st cousin' (literally: one-generation marriage-sibling)
- *totakusyb* = '2nd cousin' (two-generation marriage-sibling)
- *sitakusyb* = '3rd cousin' (three-generation marriage-sibling)

Compound relationships combine fused forms with neutral roots:
- *saisyb* = 'aunt or uncle' (parent-sibling, gender unspecified)
- *paisyb* = 'paternal aunt or uncle' (father-sibling, gender unspecified)
- *paicyb* = 'paternal aunt' (father-sister, both gendered)
- *maihyb* = 'maternal uncle' (mother-brother, both gendered)
